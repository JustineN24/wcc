Dr. Denise Faustman

A diabetes treatment based on one that cured mice in laboratory experiments has produced promising results in humans enrolled in an initial drug trial, killing immune system cells that attack diabetics’ pancreases and raising levels of a chemical marker that indicates that the pancreas is producing insulin, according to the trial’s lead researcher.

"It’s promising, but it’s not replacing insulin yet."

Interesting Facts

  1. Associate Professor of Medicine Denise Faustman, is a physician at Harvard-affiliated Massachusetts General Hospital
  2. Dr. Denise Faustman is a type 1 diabetes researcher
  3. Faustman and her research team stunned the diabetes world and generated a storm of controversy 10 years ago when she announced that she’d cured diabetes in laboratory mice with advanced disease.
  4. She has now discovered a new type 1 diabetes treatment based on a decades-old tuberculosis vaccine, which has produced promising results in humans.
